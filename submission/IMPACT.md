# GrantScribe — Impact (Slack Agent for Good)

## The problem
Money for opportunity already exists — billions in federal grants, thousands of scholarships, and a
world of free, high-quality learning material. The barrier isn't availability; it's **access**. The
organizations and people who need it most are the least equipped to get it:

- A small nonprofit serving refugee kids has no grant-writer. Finding the right federal grant means
  wading through hundreds of loosely-matched listings on grants.gov, then writing an application from
  a blank page — so they don't apply, and the money goes to better-resourced organizations.
- A first-generation student can't afford a college counselor, doesn't know which scholarships fit,
  and freezes at the essay.
- A parent who can't pay for tutoring doesn't know that a free, peer-reviewed textbook and a full
  free course already exist for exactly what their kid needs.

The result is a quiet, compounding inequality: opportunity flows to whoever already has the staff,
the counselor, or the know-how to ask for it.

## What GrantScribe does
GrantScribe meets people **where they already work and talk — Slack** — and removes the access
barrier in three ways, all from one plain-language request:

1. **Grants** for nonprofits — finds the open federal grants they actually qualify for and **drafts
   the Letter of Intent in the organization's own voice**, grounded in their past reports.
2. **Scholarships** for students — finds matching scholarships and drafts the application essay in
   the student's voice.
3. **Free learning resources** for anyone — surfaces free, level-appropriate textbooks and courses
   (Internet Archive, OpenStax, MIT OpenCourseWare, Khan Academy) so cost is never the reason a
   person can't learn something.

## Why it matters
It does the two things under-resourced people can't do for themselves: **the searching and the
first draft.** It turns "I don't have a grant-writer / counselor / money for tutoring" into "here's
what you qualify for, and here's the application, in your words." The voice-matched draft is the
difference between a tool that *lists* opportunities and one that actually gets a real application
out the door.

## Who it reaches
Every small nonprofit without development staff; every first-gen or low-income student; every parent
or self-learner priced out of education. The impact compounds beyond Slack: each funded grant is a
program that runs, each scholarship is a student enrolled, each free resource is a barrier removed —
distributing opportunity to the people the current system overlooks.

**GrantScribe's mission in one sentence: make sure the only thing standing between people and
opportunity is *whether it exists* — never whether they could find it or ask for it.**
