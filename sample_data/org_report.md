# New Roots Tutoring Collective — 2024–2025 Program & Grant Report

**Submitted to:** The Franklin County Community Foundation
**Prepared by:** Amara Okonkwo, Executive Director

## Who We Are
New Roots Tutoring Collective is a Columbus, Ohio nonprofit founded in 2019 that
provides free after-school tutoring, English-language support, and mentoring to
refugee and immigrant youth in grades 3–12. We serve students from Bhutanese,
Somali, Congolese, and Afghan families, most of whom arrived in central Ohio
within the last five years and are the first in their families to navigate U.S.
public schools.

## What We Did This Year
With your $40,000 community grant, we operated three neighborhood tutoring sites
hosted in partnership with Columbus City Schools and the Ethiopian Tewahedo
Social Services resettlement agency. In the 2024–2025 school year we:

- Served **142 students** across our Northland, Hilltop, and Whitehall sites.
- Delivered **6,800+ hours** of one-on-one and small-group tutoring.
- Matched **61 trained volunteer tutors** with students, supported by four
  part-time bilingual program coordinators.
- Saw **88% of our students promoted to the next grade**, and helped 19 high
  school seniors complete college or trade-school applications.

## What We Learned
Our families consistently tell us the after-school hours are when learning gaps
either close or widen. Demand has outgrown our volunteer model: waitlists at all
three sites now exceed 30 students. Our coordinators — the people who keep tutors
trained and families engaged — are funded entirely on soft money, and we cannot
sustain them past the current year without new operating support.

## What's Next
We aim to add a fourth site in the Morse Road corridor and convert two coordinator
roles from part-time to full-time, so we can serve an additional 60 students and
stop turning families away. Our greatest need is **flexible operating funding for
staff salaries and learning supplies** — the unglamorous core that makes
everything else possible.

Thank you for believing that a child's first years in a new country should be a
beginning, not a barrier.
